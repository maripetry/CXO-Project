About this document

FBA is at an inflection point. A strong financial base, a bold 5-year plan, and a Brazilian acquisition create the conditions for global transformation — if the right platform partner is in place.

This document maps FBA's 4 Pillars of transformation to Salesforce capabilities, contextualizes each executive's priorities with their 2030 targets, and outlines a clear path to engagement.

What this is not

Context & Challenges

The FBA Opportunity

FBA is a full-service bank with 13.5M retail customers and 900K SME clients across Europe, North America, and Brazil. The recent Brazilian acquisition positions FBA to use LATAM as an innovation incubator for global transformation. Strong financials and a board-aligned 5-year plan provide the mandate. The challenge is execution at scale.

4 Pillars of Transformation

  • Scale & Growth: Expand in Europe/North America, drive new growth in LATAM with agility
  • Macroeconomic Adaptation: Respond faster to market forces, improve profitability levers
  • AI & Innovation: Match customer expectations set by neobanks and embedded finance
  • Customer Experience: Deliver personalized, responsive journeys across all channels

Salesforce Footprint Today (Banco FBA)

Salesforce is already embedded in Brazil. Financial Services Cloud serves 530 users in Call Centre and Credit Card Operations. Marketing Cloud powers personalized offer management. MuleSoft integrates legacy systems. Current licence value: $650K — agreement ends January 2027.

This is the foundation for global expansion, not a standalone deployment.

The window
The January 2027 renewal is the natural moment to shift from vendor to strategic partner and extend the platform globally. The conversation needs to start now.

Fragmented Customer View Across Channels & Geographies
CCOCFOCIOCOO

With 13.5M retail and 900K SME customers across three geographies and multiple legacy systems, no one has a single, actionable view. Every team — commercial, ops, IT, finance — is working from a partial picture.

SME & Private Segment Growth — $65B Mortgage + 20% SME Lending by 2030
CCO

The SME portfolio is the highest-growth segment — but acquisition and cross-sell are not yet data-driven. Reaching 20% annual SME lending growth and $65B in mortgage lending requires knowing which product fits which customer, at scale, at the right moment.

Branch Network Transformation — 1,200 Branches, New Role
CCOCOO

Branches built for transactions need to become advisory hubs. Reaching 16M+ customers while lowering cost-to-income to 40% requires both commercial uplift and operational efficiency from the same network — simultaneously.

Omnichannel Journey Continuity
CCOCOO

Customers move between digital and physical without continuity. A customer who starts on the app and walks into a branch is starting from scratch. Every broken handoff is a lost conversion and an avoidable cost.

Board-Level Performance & Governance Visibility — +91% Net Profit Target
CFO

Tony is pushing for real-time insight into performance, risk, and governance across all markets. Today's reporting is retrospective and siloed. With net profit targets of +91% and net interest margin doubling to 2.5%, the Board needs forward-looking visibility — not end-of-quarter summaries.

$250M Program Investment Tracking & ROI
CFOCIO

Tony controls the budget; Richard controls the platform decisions. Both need structured investment tracking and clear ROI measurement tied to the 2030 targets. Without connecting spend to outcomes, a $250M program risks becoming a cost centre without a narrative.

Multi-Geography Regulatory Compliance
CFOCIO

GDPR (Europe), Open Finance (Brazil), and North American financial regulation represent three distinct frameworks. Manual compliance at this scale is unsustainable. Automation, audit trails, and centralized data governance are the path forward.

M&A Integration Speed & Scalability
CFOCIO

The Brazilian acquisition is the first, not the last. A common CRM and engagement platform reduces integration cost and time-to-value for each future deal. Salesforce has integrated 1,000+ financial institutions globally — this capability is built in, not built from scratch each time.

New Frontier Banking Platform Architecture
CIO

Richard's mandate is to modernize the Banking Core toward an API-first, cloud-native platform. The right CRM partner is one whose architecture is designed for integration — and whose roadmap aligns with where banking technology is heading, not where it's been.

AI in Production — Closing the Lab-to-Live Gap
CIOCOOCCO

The AI Leadership Group has organizational commitment. But AI projects routinely stall before production. The gap is integration: AI needs clean, connected operational data to deliver real impact. The right platform embeds AI in workflows — it doesn't sit alongside them.

Cost-to-Income: 50.5% → 40% by 2030 — Pillar C
COO

John's headline target is a 10-point reduction in cost-to-income ratio. The lever is meaningful, measurable process automation across branches, call centres, and back office — reducing high-cost manual interactions without degrading the customer experience.

Front & Back Office Digitization
COO

Call centre agents lack a unified customer view — high handle time, low first-call resolution. Back office is disconnected from front office decisions. Digitizing both with AI-assisted workflows is where John's operational leverage lives.

SWOT Analysis

Strategic landscape for the Salesforce partnership opportunity.

Strengths

  • ✓ Established Salesforce footprint in Banco FBA (530+ users)
  • ✓ Proven MuleSoft integration layer already in place
  • ✓ Strong financials and board-aligned 5-year plan
  • ✓ Brazilian market as AI and innovation incubator
  • ✓ CXO commitment to the 4 Pillars program
  • ✓ January 2027 renewal — a natural strategic inflection point

Weaknesses

  • ⚠ Salesforce limited to Brazil — not yet a global platform
  • ⚠ Legacy core banking dependencies creating complexity
  • ⚠ Three geographies, three regulatory frameworks
  • ⚠ Change management challenge at scale across diverse markets
  • ⚠ AI in experimentation — not yet embedded in production

Opportunities

  • → Global rollout of Salesforce across all FBA markets
  • → Brazil as test market for AI, embedded finance, open banking
  • → SME and Gen Z segments — underserved, highest-value
  • → M&A integration platform for future acquisitions
  • → Branches as advisory hubs with Salesforce as intelligence layer
  • → Real-time Board-level performance and governance visibility

Threats

  • ✕ Microsoft and others actively engaged at C-level
  • ✕ Neobanks capturing share with digital-first models
  • ✕ Multiple partners in competitive evaluation
  • ✕ Rapid AI evolution — window to lead may close quickly
  • ✕ Multi-geography execution risk across complex org structure

Why Salesforce

Partner, Not Vendor

FBA's CXOs are clear: the partner they choose must minimize execution risk, not just deliver software. That means global scale, a financial services track record, and a model built around outcomes — not licences.

Salesforce Answers the 4 Pillars

Scale & Growth

Financial Services Cloud is built for complex, multi-segment banking at scale. From SME relationship management to private client advisory, it gives Hilary the commercial layer to reach 16M+ customers and hit the $65B mortgage target.

Macroeconomic Adaptation

Einstein Analytics and real-time dashboards give Tony and the Board live performance and risk visibility — shifting reporting from retrospective to predictive, and connecting the $250M investment to the 2030 targets.

AI & Innovation

Einstein AI is embedded across all clouds — not bolted on. It operationalizes AI in the workflows where Richard's teams already work, closing the gap between the AI Leadership Group's ambitions and production reality.

Customer Experience

Customer 360 creates a single view across every channel — branch, app, contact centre. Hilary's advisors see the full picture before every conversation. John's agents handle calls with context, not cold starts.

Operational Efficiency

Service Cloud and Flow automation eliminate manual processes across contact centres, branches, and back office. John's 40% cost-to-income target has a clear technology path — with measurable milestones along the way.

M&A Ready

Salesforce has integrated 1,000+ financial institutions globally. When the next acquisition happens, a common platform reduces integration from years to months — protecting the investment thesis from day one.

Competitive edge
Salesforce is the #1 CRM in LATAM. The Brazil footprint is not a proof of concept — it's a launchpad. The January 2027 renewal is the moment to expand it globally, not renegotiate it locally.
